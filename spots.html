1. Ben Hill Griffin Stadium

Ben Hill Griffin Stadium stands proud as the most recognizable place on campus on Gale Lemerand Drive and Stadium Road. When not packed to the teeth on Gamedays the stadium is popular spot for students to workout and study. Next time you are out running stadiums, take a break and enjoy a nice power-nap on the fields soft turf for an energy booster!

Nap Setting: 6

Shade: 6

Comfort: 5

Noise: 4

2. Florida Gymnasium

Florida Gymnasium is located to the right of Ben Hill and across from Weimer Hall on Stadium Road. The right side of the 3rd floor is a hallway leading to lecture halls. Featured in this hallway are individual cubbies against the windows with spectacular views, outlets, and plenty of leg room. Charge your laptop and catch up on that sleep you missed before heading to your MMC3260 lecture.

Nap Setting: 7

Shade: 2

Comfort: 5

Noise: 1

3. Library West

Trying to take a nap, but would like to get face-time while doing so? Library West is located on University Ave and north of the Plaza of Americas. Here you can take a break from the grind and nap in one of the many cushioned chairs for all your facey friends to see! With 4 floors to choose from you can't go wrong.

Nap Setting: 2

Shade: 3

Comfort: 6

Noise: 10

4. Plaza of the America's

You feel the need for a power nap while at the library but want to escape the noise? The Plaza of America's is directly south of Library West and directly north of University Auditorium. Here you can easily set up your hammock or enu between the trees and slumber against the backdrop of students walking to class and birds chirping in the trees. Warning: if you do not like the smell from the smelly Indian food being served at lunch stay to the east side of the plaza.

Nap Setting: 6

Shade: 8

Comfort: 7

Noise: 5

5. University Auditorium

One of UF's best kept secrets, the University Auditorium is arguably the best nap spot on campus. Located right next to Century Tower and east of Turlington Square the Auditorium should be your next napping destination. The front half of the building features 3 floors, each with multiple cushioned benches, dimmed lighting, and fantastic views. Pick your spot and rest your spirit while soft orchestra music by the UF band plays in the background.

Nap Setting: 10

Shade: 9

Comfort: 9

Noise: 1

6. Marston Library

Trying to get your Zzz's in but not feeling facey? Marston library located in Turlington Square is a great place to get a nap in while at the library. The 4th and 5th floors are silent study areas where you can rest your head against a desk and realize your breaths are the louder than the intellectuals studying around you.

Nap Setting: 6

Shade: 1

Comfort: 3

Noise: 2

7. Reitz Union North Lawn

The North Lawn covers the most area on campus and is located south of Stadium Road and North of Museum road. Here you can have the classic college nap under one the many shade trees and be close to the Reitz Union and Hub for an after-nap snack.

Environment: 8

Shade: 9

Comfort: 6

Noise: 4

8. Reitz Union

The Reitz Union located off Museum Road and south of Weimer Hall is the center of student life at UF. The Reitz has comfy chairs and couches on every floor and easy access to restrooms, water fountains, and food.

Nap Setting: 6

Shade: 6

Comfort: 7

Noise: 4

9. Flavet Field

Flavet Field located south of Stadium Road and east of Fraternity Row is a grassy field with spectacular views. Imagine yourself in a happy slumber on the plush grass next to that special someone you just had a Graham Oasis picnic with. Whether you prefer to nap in the field by the Quidditch players or under the shaded trees surrounding Flavet you will be hard pressed to find a more relaxing environment this side of campus.

Environment: 7

Shade: 6

Comfort: 6

Noise: 4
